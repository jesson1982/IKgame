Troop Synergy & Composition

Understanding how different troop types work together is essential for successful attacks. The right composition can mean the difference between victory and defeat.

Rock-Paper-Scissors System:

  • Infantry beats Rangers: Infantry tanks ranger damage effectively
  • Rangers beat Cavalry: Rangers outrange and destroy cavalry
  • Cavalry beats Infantry: Cavalry flanks and overwhelms infantry

2 Recommended Attack Compositions

Balanced Attack Army

  • 40% Infantry: Frontline to absorb initial damage
  • 35% Rangers: Main damage dealers from behind
  • 25% Cavalry: Flanking force and cleanup crew

Best for: General purpose attacks against unknown defenses

Aggressive DPS Army

  • 20% Infantry: Minimal tanking
  • 50% Rangers: Maximum ranged damage
  • 30% Cavalry: Heavy flanking pressure

Best for: Attacking weaker targets you expect to win quickly

Siege Army

  • 50% Infantry: Heavy frontline for prolonged engagement
  • 30% Rangers: Sustained damage output
  • 20% Siege: Building destruction specialists

Best for: Destroying enemy buildings and infrastructure

💡 Composition Tip

  • Always scout your target first to adjust composition accordingly
  • Match your troop type to counter the defender's main troop type
  • Higher tier troops dramatically outperform lower tiers

2 Scouting Before Attack

Never attack without proper reconnaissance!

  • Send Scouts: Always scout to see defender's troops
  • Check Power: Compare your power to target's power
  • Wall Level: Higher walls mean longer battle time
  • Garrison Heroes: Note which heroes are defending
  • Alliance Status: Check if target has active alliance
  • Shield Status: Verify target doesn't have active shield

⚠️ Important

Attacking without scouting is gambling! You might walk into a trap or face unexpectedly strong defenses. Always scout first.

3 When NOT to Attack

  • Your army is still recovering from previous battles
  • Target has significantly higher power (2x or more)
  • Target is in a strong alliance that will retaliate
  • You're low on resources to train replacement troops
  • Your best heroes are injured or tired
  • You have an active shield (attacking removes it)

Rally Coordination

Rallies allow multiple players to combine forces for devastating attacks. Proper coordination is key to successful rallies.

1 Starting a Rally

  • Select Target: Choose a high-value target worth the effort
  • Set Rally Size: Larger rallies attract more participants
  • Timing: Start rallies when alliance members are online
  • Communication: Announce rally in alliance chat
  • Hero Assignment: Use your best attack hero as rally leader

2 Joining Rallies

  • Quick Response: Join rallies quickly before they fill up
  • Appropriate Troops: Send troops matching the rally's needs
  • Hero Support: Use supportive heroes even if not leading
  • Don't Overcommit: Keep some troops for personal defense
  • Watch Timer: Rally departure time is fixed - be ready

3 Rally Best Practices

  • Coordinate Timing: Ensure key members are available
  • Mix Troop Types: Different participants bring variety
  • Target Selection: Focus on strategic objectives
  • Post-Attack Plan: Have follow-up actions ready
  • Share Rewards: Distribute loot fairly among participants

💡 Rally Tip

  • The rally leader's hero skills affect the entire rally force
  • Choose rally leaders with strong attack buffs
  • Larger rallies deal exponentially more damage than solo attacks

Battle Timing & March Speed

1 Optimal Attack Times

  • Enemy Offline: Attack when defenders can't respond
  • Time Zone Advantage: Attack during enemy's nighttime
  • After Their Battles: Strike when their army is weakened
  • During Events: Some events provide attack bonuses
  • Coordinated Strikes: Multiple attacks overwhelm defenses

2 March Speed Optimization

  • Research Speed Tech: Priority research for faster marches
  • Hero Skills: Some heroes increase march speed
  • Items: Use speed-up items for critical attacks
  • Distance Matters: Closer targets arrive faster
  • Terrain Effects: Some terrain slows movement

3 Multi-Wave Attacks

For serious assaults, coordinate multiple attack waves:

  • Wave 1: Weakening attack to drain defender's troops
  • Wave 2: Follow-up attack while defender is recovering
  • Wave 3: Final assault to achieve objectives
  • Timing: Space waves to prevent full recovery
  • Coordination: Requires alliance-wide communication
